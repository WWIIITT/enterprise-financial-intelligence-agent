# Investment Research Review Policy

## Policy Owner

Research Supervision and Compliance.

## Purpose

This policy defines review requirements for investment research content generated or assisted by AI systems, analysts, data tools, or automated research workflows.

## Scope

The policy applies to internal research notes, company analysis, macro commentary, valuation risk summaries, sector comparisons, and any material that may support an investment decision or external client communication.

## Research Evidence Requirements

Research outputs must clearly distinguish between facts, assumptions, estimates, and opinions. Claims about company performance must cite supporting sources such as SEC filings, earnings releases, approved financial databases, or internal models. Macro claims must identify the economic series, observation period, and data source.

## AI-Assisted Research

AI-assisted research is permitted when:

- The AI system uses approved data sources.
- Citations are provided for document-based claims.
- A human reviewer verifies factual accuracy.
- The output is not distributed externally until the required review is completed.

AI-generated text must not be copied into final research without review for hallucinations, unsupported claims, outdated data, and missing risk disclosures.

## Review and Approval

Before distribution, research must be reviewed for:

- Source quality and citation accuracy.
- Reasonableness of assumptions.
- Consistency with approved methodology.
- Conflicts of interest.
- Required risk language.
- Compliance with client communication standards.

High-impact research or research involving sensitive sectors requires senior reviewer approval.

## Recordkeeping

The final research package must retain source evidence, reviewer identity, approval timestamp, material revisions, and AI system trace when AI assistance is used.
